​1. System Architecture & Spatial Logic
​The NetZero RS-1 Kinetic Core is a modular, high-efficiency mechanical battery framework designed to eliminate the degradation, toxic footprint, and resource monopolies of traditional chemical energy storage. By shifting the energy storage medium to pure motion, this architecture is built to provide independent power for generations.
​Key Mechanical Innovations:
​Net-Zero Torque Matrix (\sum\tau = 0): Traditional flywheel systems suffer from massive gyroscopic precession forces that stress mounts and make mobile deployment impossible. The NetZero RS-1 solves this by locking kinetic rotors into a rigid, rectangular composite chassis in alternating pairs (A_1 clockwise, B_1 counter-clockwise). Because they share the same solid frame, their opposing angular momentum vectors cancel out perfectly, creating a completely stable, neutral outer enclosure.
​Zero-Drag Vacuum Sleeves: To eliminate aerodynamic drag and thermal breakdown, each rotor mass spins within a hermetically sealed polycarbonate or composite sleeve evacuated to a deep vacuum (< 10^{-5}\text{ Torr}).
​Contactless MagLev Suspension: The rotors utilize passive magnetic levitation (MagLev) cradles at the upper and lower seals. By completely floating the spinning mass, mechanical bearing wear and physical friction are reduced to zero.
​Stationary Electromagnetic Induction: Power transfer is completely wireless. External copper induction windings are wrapped around a stationary ferrite form outside the vacuum sleeve. These interface electromagnetically with permanent neodymium magnets embedded in the rotor caps, acting as a brushless motor during charging and a generator during power draw.
